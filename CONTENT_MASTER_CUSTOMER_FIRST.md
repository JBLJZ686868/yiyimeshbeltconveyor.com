# YIYI Mesh Belt Website Content Master

## Positioning
YIYI Mesh Belt should not sound like a generic export catalog supplier.
It should read like a factory-backed industrial partner for OEM builders, distributors, and replacement buyers who need the belt to fit, run, and arrive predictably.

Core promise:

`Engineered to fit. Verified before shipment. Reliable for repeat supply.`

Core proof:

- Robotic welding
- In-house wire drawing
- Drum / fit verification for critical projects
- Material certificate + dimensional report + QA evidence
- Permanent specification retention for repeat orders

Core inquiry triggers:

- "Send Drawing"
- "Request a Quote"
- "Start Replacement Review"
- "Talk to an Engineer"

## Buyer Segments
### OEM Equipment Builders
What they care about:

- Will the belt match the system before production starts?
- Can the supplier confirm drum diameter, pitch, running direction, and edge style?
- Will the project stay on schedule?
- Can they trust the QC evidence before balance payment?

Message angle:

`We do not just supply belts. We help your conveyor project fit, run, and ship with fewer surprises.`

### Distributors
What they care about:

- Can they present the supplier confidently to their customer?
- Can they reorder without restarting the technical discussion?
- Can they get documentation fast?
- Will the supplier support urgent replacements?

Message angle:

`Your reputation depends on what you supply. We make sure you have something worth backing.`

### Replacement Buyers
What they care about:

- Can the new belt match the existing system?
- Does the supplier need a sample, photos, or dimensions?
- Can the supplier identify hidden mismatch risks before production?
- Can urgent orders be handled?

Message angle:

`If the old belt failed, the next one has to match more than width alone.`

## Site-Wide Voice Rules
- Write from the buyer's risk, not from factory pride.
- Replace "we have many products" with "here is why your order is safer here."
- Use practical industrial language, not decorative marketing language.
- Every major page should answer:
  - Why trust this supplier?
  - Why inquire now?
  - What should the buyer send next?
