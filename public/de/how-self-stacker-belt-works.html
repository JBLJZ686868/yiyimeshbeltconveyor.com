How a Self-Stacker Belt Works

A self-stacker belt is built for spiral-style conveying where the belt structure and drive relationship allow stable stacked movement. It is not just another mesh belt shape; it is a system-specific belt that depends on the right geometry and matched production details.

See Self-Stacker Belt PageGet Engineering Support
Why It Matters
  • Self-stacker systems are less forgiving of geometry mismatch
  • OEM projects need matched production, not generic substitution
  • Replacement jobs often require reverse review from photos or old samples

Key Logic

  • The belt must maintain stable stacked travel
  • Drive relationship affects how reliably the system runs
  • Matched specification reduces field instability

Common Mistakes

  • Substituting from a broad catalog without system review
  • Ignoring old-belt evidence during replacement
  • Separating the belt decision from its drive conditions
