Spiral Freezer Belt Production Process

A spiral freezer belt is not only a belt. In an IQF or spiral tower system, belt pitch, edge structure, drum diameter, sprocket engagement, and low-temperature stability all affect installation risk. This guide explains how YIYI reviews and produces spiral freezer belts for OEM and replacement projects.

Problem to Verification

Where Spiral Belt Risk Usually Starts

Most project risk appears before production: incomplete drawings, unclear edge clearance, wrong drum diameter, unmatched drive parts, or material requirements that do not match the freezing environment.

Project Risk

Small pitch, width, rod, edge, or sprocket errors can cause tracking problems, installation delay, or unstable running in the spiral tower.

Engineering Check

YIYI reviews drawing data, old belt photos, sample references, drum diameter, turning direction, material grade, and application temperature.

Verified Mechanism

Material control, forming, robotic welding support, in-process inspection, drum test support, and final QA reduce mismatch risk before shipment.

Production Flow

From Drawing Review to Export Packing

1. Drawing Review

Confirm belt width, pitch, rod diameter, wire diameter, edge style, material, and system conditions.

2. Material Control

Check stainless material, wire condition, and incoming material consistency before forming and assembly.

3. Forming and Welding

Control mesh structure, rods, side edges, and welding consistency for repeatable spiral belt geometry.

4. Drum Test Support

Where required, running or drum-related verification helps confirm practical fit before shipment.

5. Final Inspection

Review width, pitch, edge finish, weld condition, surface condition, and packing readiness.

6. Export Delivery

Prepare protected packing and export coordination for OEM builders, distributors, and replacement projects.
