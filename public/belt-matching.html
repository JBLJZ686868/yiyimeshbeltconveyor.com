Photo Guide

3 Photos Are All We Need

Use your phone. Each shot gives us a specific measurement or structural detail. Together, three photos are enough to identify the full specification of any standard metal conveyor belt.

Shot 01 - Top View
Top-Down View of the Weave
Place the belt flat. Photograph straight down, filling the frame with the belt surface. This shows the spiral and rod pitch - the two most critical identification parameters for any metal conveyor belt.
✓Spiral Pitch · Rod Pitch · Weave Pattern
Shot 02 - Side Edge
Side Edge - Close Up
Photograph the belt edge directly side-on, close enough to see the link and rod structure clearly. This shows edge treatment type (button welded, tucked, loop) and link height - both critical for drum fit.
✓Edge Type · Link Height · Wire Diameter
Shot 03 - Width
Width with Tape Measure
Lay a tape measure across the full belt width - edge to edge. Photograph so the measurement markings and both belt edges are visible. This confirms belt width for frame fit on installation.
✓Belt Width · Overall Dimensions
🔍
Optional but fastest: Cut a 30cm section of your worn belt and courier it to our factory. We measure every dimension in our lab - spiral pitch, rod pitch, wire diameter, link thickness, edge construction - and confirm the spec the same day the sample arrives. Contact us for the shipping address.
Specification Parameters

Everything We Confirm From Your Photos

Every parameter below is identified and confirmed in writing before sample review or production. Nothing assumed - everything measured.

Parameter 01
Spiral Pitch
Distance between consecutive spiral wires across the belt width. Primary identification parameter - determines drive engagement with the drum and defines the belt's structural category.
Range: 8 · 10 · 12 · 14 · 16mm
Parameter 02
Rod Pitch
Spacing between cross rods along the belt length. Determines flexibility, product support surface, and drive-fit behaviour. Critical for correct drive-fit verification.
Range: 25 · 31.75 · 38 · 50mm
Parameter 03
Wire Diameter
Diameter of spiral wire and cross rod. Determines belt weight, load capacity, and minimum bend radius. YIYI Mesh Belt draws wire in-house from 1.0mm to 12mm - wire diameter is confirmed against the required specification before production.
Range: 1.2 · 1.6 · 2.0 · 2.5 · 3.2mm
Parameter 04
Edge Treatment
How the belt edge is finished. Affects belt tracking, side guide contact, and drive system compatibility. Identified from the side edge photo - the most visually distinctive parameter.
Button Welded · Tucked · Loop · Welded Bar
Parameter 05
Belt Width
Overall width from edge to edge. Measured from your photos or physical sample. Confirmed against your conveyor frame measurement before production begins.
Custom: 200mm to 3,000mm+
Parameter 06
Material Grade
SUS304, SUS316L, or carbon steel. Identified from your application environment. Food-processing defaults to 304; aggressive chlorine or salt environments require 316L. Confirmed before production.
SUS304 · SUS316L · Carbon · POM Hybrid

FAQ

Belt Matching - Common Questions

My belt came from a Western OEM system. Can you still match it?+
Yes. We regularly match belts from all major IQF, tunnel oven, spiral cooler, and general conveyor OEM platforms. The belt specification is physical - it is defined by wire diameter, pitch, and weave pattern, not by who originally manufactured it. As long as the belt is a standard metal spiral, eye link, flat wire, or weave construction, we can match it from photos.
How accurate is photo-based identification?+
For standard belt constructions - spiral freezer, eye link, self-stacking, flat wire - photo identification is accurate for belt type, approximate pitch, and edge treatment. Wire diameter is estimated from photos and confirmed by physical measurement or sample review before full production. We never proceed to production without confirmed specifications.
What if the sample does not fit my system?+
If the sample does not fit correctly - wrong pitch, width, or drum engagement - we adjust the specification before production. The matching service does not end until the specification is confirmed for your system.
My belt is heavily worn. Can you still identify the spec?+
Yes. Even heavily worn belts retain enough structural detail for identification. The spiral weave pattern, rod spacing, and wire diameter are visible even on belts at end-of-life. If the belt is too deformed for photo identification, a physical 30cm section sent to our factory allows direct measurement regardless of surface condition.
Is there any charge for the matching service or sample review?+
Specification identification is part of our engineering support. If sample review is required, we confirm the sample scope, cost, and lead time clearly before proceeding. No hidden fees at any stage.
How long does the full process take from photo to belt delivered?+
Specification review starts from your photos and measurements. Sample production, when required, usually takes 7-10 working days. Your test-fit may take 1-2 working days. Full production: 15-25 working days. Air freight delivery: 3-7 days depending on destination. Total from first photo to belt installed depends on confirmation speed and logistics. Emergency compressed timelines available - contact us directly.
