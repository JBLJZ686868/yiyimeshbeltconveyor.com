HYGIENE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Eye Link Belt & Food Safety - Engineering for Hygiene

For food processing OEMs designing to EU and US food safety standards, belt selection is a hygiene design decision, not just a mechanical one.

OPEN AREA & CLEANABILITY

Why Open Area Matters for Hygiene

High open area in eye link belt serves two hygiene functions: it allows complete drainage of wash water and processing liquids, eliminating standing water that creates bacterial growth conditions; and it enables cleaning water and sanitiser to reach all surfaces of the belt in a single CIP pass. Dense mesh belts with low open area require multiple cleaning passes and still retain moisture in recesses.

ELECTRO-POLISHING

Surface Finish & Biofilm Prevention

Standard wire drawing typically leaves a surface roughness of about Ra 0.8 to 1.2 µm. At that level, bacterial cells around 0.5 to 2.0 µm can colonise surface microstructures. Electro-polishing reduces surface roughness to below Ra 0.4 µm, lowering the risk of stable biofilm formation. For premium food safety environments, YIYI Mesh Belt offers electro-polished SUS316L eye link belts as a standard production option.

SUS316L vs SUS304 - THE CASE FOR MOLYBDENUM

Why Seafood and Meat Processing Lines Use SUS316L

SUS304 is an excellent food-grade stainless steel - but it has a specific weakness in chloride environments. Chloride ions (from seawater, brine, meat processing liquids, or sodium hypochlorite CIP sanitiser) attack the passive oxide layer on SUS304 and can initiate pitting corrosion. In a food processing environment, pits in belt wire become contamination traps that are effectively impossible to clean.

SUS316L adds roughly 2% to 3% molybdenum to the alloy composition. Molybdenum strengthens the passive oxide layer specifically against chloride attack, dramatically reducing pitting corrosion susceptibility. For any application involving brine, seawater, seafood, meat, or regular chlorine sanitisation, SUS316L is the correct specification. YIYI Mesh Belt stocks SUS316L wire in standard diameters and verifies grade by alloy spectrometer test on every batch.

INSTALLATION GUIDANCE

Installing Eye Link Belt - Washing & Pasteurisation Lines

Application-specific installation guidance for washing, pasteurisation, and seafood processing lines.

01
Passivation After Installation

New SUS316L eye link belts should be passivated after installation to restore the protective oxide layer disturbed during cutting and joining. Use a food-grade passivation solution, typically 20-25% nitric acid or a citric-acid alternative, per ASTM A967 or equivalent. This step significantly extends service life in aggressive washing environments.

02
Initial Tension Setting for Wet Applications

Eye link belts running through wash tunnels should be tensioned at operating temperature with water present on the belt. Dry tensioning at room temperature will result in over-tension when the belt is wet and under full load in the wash zone - this accelerates edge bar wear and reduces service life.

03
CIP Concentration Limits

SUS316L eye link belts tolerate standard CIP concentrations of sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) up to 200 ppm free chlorine at ambient temperature. For higher concentrations or elevated temperature CIP (>60°C), rinse immediately after sanitiser contact. Extended contact with concentrated hypochlorite at elevated temperature can cause surface discolouration and reduces the passive oxide layer protection over time.

04
Drainage Design Around Belt Support

For washing lines, ensure belt support rails or slider beds allow free drainage of wash water. Standing water on belt support surfaces between the belt passes is a hygiene risk. Our engineering team can advise on belt support design for new washing line installations to eliminate standing water zones.

Industry Problems →YIYI Mesh Belt Solutions

Why Eye Link Belts Fail in Washing & Pasteurisation Lines - And How YIYI Mesh Belt Solves Each Root Cause

Verified failure patterns from food processing operators across Europe, North America, and Southeast Asia. This is what we see in replacement inquiries.

Issue 01 - Industry Problem
Corrosion at Link Joints Under Repeated CIP Washdown
Eye link belts in seafood and meat washing lines are subject to aggressive chlorine-based CIP cycles. Standard SUS304 belts show crevice corrosion at link-to-link contact zones, where trapped chloride ions attack the passive oxide layer. Pitting failure typically begins at these contact points.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
SUS316L Standard for Washdown Applications - Molybdenum Barrier
YIYI Mesh Belt standard for seafood and meat washing is SUS316L - the 2.0 - .5% molybdenum addition provides a direct barrier against chloride ion attack at crevice zones. We confirm alloy composition via gun testing before production. For applications with unusual CIP chemistry, we provide compatibility confirmation before quoting.
Issue 02 - Industry Problem
Link Stretch Under Sustained Tension - Belt Pitch Elongates Over Time
Under-spec wire diameter causes progressive link stretch in high-tension washing line applications. As pitch increases, the belt no longer engages the drive sprocket correctly, accelerating wear and eventually causing slip or jamming at the drive end.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
In-House Wire Drawing - Tensile Strength Verified Before Production
YIYI Mesh Belt draws all wire in-house on our USD 400,000 drawing line. Wire tensile strength is tested on our Brinell tester before any production begins. We calculate minimum wire diameter from your conveyor geometry, belt speed, and maximum product load - not from catalogue defaults. This eliminates under-spec wire as a failure cause.
Issue 03 - Industry Problem
Product Catching in Mesh - Food Safety and Belt Damage
Eye link belts with rough rod ends or protruding weld spatter create product catch points. Small products like shrimp, diced meat, and shellfish can lodge in rough areas, causing food safety issues and accelerating belt wear at trapped product zones.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
Flush Rod Ends + Electro-Polishing Option for HACCP Compliance
YIYI Mesh Belt standard edge treatment produces flush rod ends with no protrusion beyond the edge bar. Electro-polishing (SUS316L) available on request - reduces surface Ra value and eliminates micro-pits that harbour bacteria. EC 1935/2004 food contact declaration available for EU customers.
Issue 04 - Industry Problem
Replacement Belt Does Not Reach the Target Open Area - Drainage Performance Drops
Replacement belts sourced to the wrong link pitch deliver lower open area than the original specification. Drainage performance drops below system design, increasing product moisture after washing and disrupting downstream process parameters.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
Open Area Calculated and Confirmed - Free Sample Before Mass Production
We calculate achieved open area from confirmed link pitch and wire diameter - this is documented in the specification sheet sent for approval before production starts. For replacement orders, we match the original specification and verify via a free sample. Open area is not an estimate - it is a calculable dimension that we hold to drawing.

Engineering-level answers for eye link belt specification, sourcing, and food compliance questions.

Can YIYI Mesh Belt supply a direct replacement for a Western or European OEM eye link belt?鈻?/span>

Yes. Provide your existing belt sample, the manufacturer's part number, or key dimensions - link pitch, rod pitch, wire diameter, belt width, and edge construction - and we will produce a dimensionally matched replacement. What makes eye link belt better than other belt types for washing and pasteurisation lines?鈻?/span>

Eye link construction delivers the highest open area of any standard wire mesh belt - typically 60 - 5% depending on pitch and wire diameter. This maximises water and steam penetration for uniform product washing, and draining between processing stages is faster with less residual moisture. For comparison, balanced weave belt achieves 35 - 0% open area, and flat wire belt under 30%. For applications where drainage speed and product contact area matter - seafood, poultry, blanching, pasteurisation - eye link is the engineered choice.

We need: belt width (inside dimension), link pitch (longitudinal), rod pitch (transverse), wire diameter, and edge construction type (welded rod end, folded edge, or fabric edge). You do not need every dimension before contacting us - send what you have and we confirm the gaps in the first engineering response.

SUS304 vs SUS316L for eye link belt - when does each apply?鈻?/span>

SUS304 is suitable for ambient food processing environments without aggressive cleaning chemicals - general conveying, dry processing, packaging. SUS316L is required for applications involving chlorine-based CIP sanitisers, salt contact (seafood), and high-moisture meat processing. The molybdenum content in 316L provides direct resistance to chloride ion attack at link-to-link contact zones where crevice corrosion initiates. If you are unsure, we recommend 316L - the cost difference is modest and the corrosion resistance improvement for washdown environments is significant.

Do you offer electro-polished eye link belts?鈻?/span>

Yes. Electro-polished SUS316L reduces surface roughness (Ra value), eliminates micro-pits where bacteria can colonise, and improves resistance to staining. It is required by some EU HACCP protocols and certain retail supply chain standards. Available on request for SUS316L eye link belts - specify at the time of order confirmation. An EC 1935/2004 food contact materials declaration is available for EU customers on request.

How do I calculate the open area percentage for my application?鈻?/span>

Open area percentage is calculated from link pitch, rod pitch, and wire diameter - it is a defined geometric property, not an estimate. If you have a minimum open area requirement (for drainage rate, steam penetration, or regulatory compliance), specify it and we will design the link pitch geometry to meet it. SUS316L resists standard CIP chemicals at normal concentrations: sodium hypochlorite (chlorine sanitiser), caustic soda (NaOH) for alkaline cleaning, phosphoric and citric acid for descaling, and peracetic acid-based sanitisers. For highly concentrated chemicals, unusual blends, or elevated CIP temperatures above 80°C, contact us with specific chemistry details. HYGIENE DESIGN PRINCIPLES

Eye Link Belt & Food Safety - Engineering for Hygiene

For food processing OEMs designing to EU and US food safety standards, belt selection is a hygiene design decision, not just a mechanical one.

OPEN AREA & CLEANABILITY

Why Open Area Matters for Hygiene

High open area in eye link belt serves two hygiene functions: it allows complete drainage of wash water and processing liquids, eliminating standing water that creates bacterial growth conditions; and it enables cleaning water and sanitiser to reach all surfaces of the belt in a single CIP pass. Dense mesh belts with low open area require multiple cleaning passes and still retain moisture in recesses.

ELECTRO-POLISHING

Surface Finish & Biofilm Prevention

Standard wire drawing leaves a Ra surface roughness of approximately 0.8 - .2 碌m. At this roughness level, bacterial cells (0.5 - 碌m diameter) can colonise surface microstructures. Electro-polishing reduces surface roughness to Ra < 0.4 碌m, below the threshold for stable biofilm formation. For premium food safety environments, YIYI Mesh Belt offers electro-polished SUS316L eye link belts as standard production.

SUS316L vs SUS304 - THE CASE FOR MOLYBDENUM

Why Seafood and Meat Processing Lines Use SUS316L

SUS304 is an excellent food-grade stainless steel - but it has a specific weakness in chloride environments. Chloride ions (from seawater, brine, meat processing liquids, or sodium hypochlorite CIP sanitiser) attack the passive oxide layer on SUS304 and can initiate pitting corrosion. In a food processing environment, pits in belt wire become contamination traps that are effectively impossible to clean.

SUS316L adds 2 - % molybdenum to the alloy composition. Molybdenum strengthens the passive oxide layer specifically against chloride attack, dramatically reducing pitting corrosion susceptibility. For any application involving brine, seawater, seafood, meat, or regular chlorine sanitisation - SUS316L is the correct specification. YIYI Mesh Belt stocks SUS316L wire in all standard diameters and verifies grade by alloy spectrometer test on every batch.

INSTALLATION GUIDANCE

Installing Eye Link Belt - Washing & Pasteurisation Lines

Application-specific installation guidance for washing, pasteurisation, and seafood processing lines.

01
Passivation After Installation

New SUS316L eye link belts should be passivated after installation to restore the protective oxide layer disturbed during cutting and joining. Use a food-grade passivation solution, typically 20-25% nitric acid or a citric-acid alternative, per ASTM A967 or equivalent. This step significantly extends service life in aggressive washing environments.

02
Initial Tension Setting for Wet Applications

Eye link belts running through wash tunnels should be tensioned at operating temperature with water present on the belt. Dry tensioning at room temperature will result in over-tension when the belt is wet and under full load in the wash zone - this accelerates edge bar wear and reduces service life.

03
CIP Concentration Limits

SUS316L eye link belts tolerate standard CIP concentrations of sodium hypochlorite (NaOCl) up to 200 ppm free chlorine at ambient temperature. For higher concentrations or elevated temperature CIP (>60°C), rinse immediately after sanitiser contact. Extended contact with concentrated hypochlorite at elevated temperature can cause surface discolouration and reduces the passive oxide layer protection over time.

04
Drainage Design Around Belt Support

For washing lines, ensure belt support rails or slider beds allow free drainage of wash water. Standing water on belt support surfaces between the belt passes is a hygiene risk. Our engineering team can advise on belt support design for new washing line installations to eliminate standing water zones.

Why Eye Link Belts Fail in Washing & Pasteurisation Lines - And How YIYI Mesh Belt Solves Each Root Cause

Verified failure patterns from food processing operators across Europe, North America, and Southeast Asia. This is what we see in replacement inquiries.

Issue 01 - Industry Problem
Corrosion at Link Joints Under Repeated CIP Washdown
Eye link belts in seafood and meat washing lines are subject to aggressive chlorine-based CIP cycles. Standard SUS304 belts show crevice corrosion at link-to-link contact zones - where trapped chloride ions attack the passive oxide layer. Pitting failure typically begins at these contact points.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
SUS316L Standard for Washdown Applications - Molybdenum Barrier
YIYI Mesh Belt standard for seafood and meat washing is SUS316L - the 2.0 - .5% molybdenum addition provides a direct barrier against chloride ion attack at crevice zones. We confirm alloy composition via gun testing before production. For applications with unusual CIP chemistry, we provide compatibility confirmation before quoting.
Issue 02 - Industry Problem
Link Stretch Under Sustained Tension - Belt Pitch Elongates Over Time
Under-spec wire diameter causes progressive link stretch in high-tension washing line applications. As pitch increases, the belt no longer engages the drive sprocket correctly - accelerating wear and eventually causing slip or jam at the drive end.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
In-House Wire Drawing - Tensile Strength Verified Before Production
YIYI Mesh Belt draws all wire in-house on our USD 400,000 drawing line. Wire tensile strength is tested on our Brinell tester before any production begins. We calculate minimum wire diameter from your conveyor geometry, belt speed, and maximum product load - not from catalogue defaults. This eliminates under-spec wire as a failure cause.
Issue 03 - Industry Problem
Product Catching in Mesh - Food Safety and Belt Damage
Eye link belts with rough rod ends or protruding weld spatter create product catch points. Small products - shrimp, diced meat, shellfish - lodge in rough areas, causing food safety issues and accelerating belt wear at trapped product zones.
✓YIYI Mesh Belt Engineering Response
Flush Rod Ends + Electro-Polishing Option for HACCP Compliance
YIYI Mesh Belt standard edge treatment produces flush rod ends with no protrusion beyond the edge bar. Electro-polishing (SUS316L) available on request - reduces surface Ra value and eliminates micro-pits that harbour bacteria. EC 1935/2004 food contact declaration available for EU customers.
Issue 04 - Industry Problem
Replacement Belt Doesn't Achieve Target Open Area - Drainage Performance Drops
Replacement belts sourced to wrong link pitch deliver lower open area than the original specification. Drainage performance drops below system design, increasing product moisture after washing and disrupting downstream process parameters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Engineering-level answers for eye link belt specification, sourcing, and food compliance questions.

Can YIYI Mesh Belt supply a direct replacement for a Western or European OEM eye link belt?▾

Yes. Provide your existing belt sample, the manufacturer's part number, or key dimensions - link pitch, rod pitch, wire diameter, belt width, and edge construction - and we will produce a dimensionally matched replacement. A free sample is produced before mass production to verify that your system's drive and drainage performance is unchanged from the original. Many of our European food processing customers have transitioned from Western-brand eye link belts for cost and lead time reasons while maintaining equivalent performance and food safety compliance.

What makes eye link belt better than other belt types for washing and pasteurisation lines?▾

Eye link construction delivers the highest open area of any standard wire mesh belt - typically 60 - 5% depending on pitch and wire diameter. This maximises water and steam penetration for uniform product washing, and draining between processing stages is faster with less residual moisture. For comparison, balanced weave belt achieves 35 - 0% open area, and flat wire belt under 30%. For applications where drainage speed and product contact area matter - seafood, poultry, blanching, pasteurisation - eye link is the engineered choice.

What information do I need to provide for an eye link belt quotation?▾

We need: belt width (inside dimension), link pitch (longitudinal), rod pitch (transverse), wire diameter, and edge construction type (welded rod end, folded edge, or fabric edge). If you have a minimum open area requirement, specify it. If you are replacing an existing belt, photos of the current belt with a ruler alongside are usually sufficient to identify the specification. You do not need every dimension before contacting us - send what you have and we confirm the gaps in the first engineering response.

SUS304 vs SUS316L for eye link belt - when does each apply?▾

SUS304 is suitable for ambient food processing environments without aggressive cleaning chemicals - general conveying, dry processing, packaging. SUS316L is required for applications involving chlorine-based CIP sanitisers, salt contact (seafood), and high-moisture meat processing. The molybdenum content in 316L provides direct resistance to chloride ion attack at link-to-link contact zones where crevice corrosion initiates. If you are unsure, we recommend 316L - the cost difference is modest and the corrosion resistance improvement for washdown environments is significant.

Do you offer electro-polished eye link belts?▾

Yes. Electro-polished SUS316L reduces surface roughness (Ra value), eliminates micro-pits where bacteria can colonise, and improves resistance to staining. It is required by some EU HACCP protocols and certain retail supply chain standards. Available on request for SUS316L eye link belts - specify at the time of order confirmation. An EC 1935/2004 food contact materials declaration is available for EU customers on request.

How do I calculate the open area percentage for my application?▾

Open area percentage is calculated from link pitch, rod pitch, and wire diameter - it is a defined geometric property, not an estimate. If you have a minimum open area requirement (for drainage rate, steam penetration, or regulatory compliance), specify it and we will design the link pitch geometry to meet it. We document achieved open area on the specification sheet sent for your approval before production begins.

What CIP cleaning chemicals is SUS316L resistant to?▾

SUS316L resists standard CIP chemicals at normal concentrations: sodium hypochlorite (chlorine sanitiser), caustic soda (NaOH) for alkaline cleaning, phosphoric and citric acid for descaling, and peracetic acid-based sanitisers. For highly concentrated chemicals, unusual blends, or elevated CIP temperatures above 80°C, contact us with specific chemistry details. We confirm compatibility before quoting rather than after delivery.

What is the expected service life of a YIYI Mesh Belt eye link belt?▾

In standard food processing washing line applications with appropriate CIP chemistry and correct material grade, YIYI Mesh Belt eye link belts are designed for 3 - 5 years of service. In-house wire drawing ensures consistent tensile properties that resist link stretch under sustained tension. Robotic welding eliminates weld-quality variation that causes early joint failure. Actual service life depends on CIP frequency and concentration, operating temperature, product load, and drive tension.
