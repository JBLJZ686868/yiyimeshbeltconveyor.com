BUYER RISK HOOK

High-Risk Belt Projects Need
Engineering Confirmation First

For OEM equipment builders and replacement buyers, the question is not only whether a belt can be made. The real risk is whether pitch, width, drum geometry, sprocket engagement, running direction, material grade, and packing requirements are confirmed before production.

01 / Pitch
Pitch Mismatch

Wrong pitch can affect sprocket engagement, tracking stability, and replacement fit.

→Built around equipment fit
02 / Width
Width and Clearance Risk

Belt width, edge clearance, and side structure must match the equipment path.

→Start from the existing system
03 / Drum
Drum Geometry Fit

Spiral and turning systems need drum diameter and contraction behavior reviewed early.

→Manufacturing proof matters
04 / Sprocket
Sprocket Engagement

Drive fit should be checked with chain pitch, roller size, bore, keyway, and layout.

→Reduce system-level risk
05 / Direction
Running Direction

Running direction and belt orientation affect installation, tracking, and repeat order accuracy.

→Designed for repeat cooperation
06 / Material
Material and Surface Risk

Material grade, surface finish, cleaning, and application-specific material requirements must be confirmed by application.
