Material Selection

Three Standard Grades — All at 1,200 MPa

All wire drawn in-house at 1,200 MPa tensile strength regardless of grade. Select based on your process chemistry and cleaning regime.

SUS304
Standard Food-Grade
General food processing, frying with clean oils, baking, coating, and cooling. Good resistance to standard cleaning agents. Most economical specification.
Frying (clean oil) Coating & breading Baking & cooling Pasteurizing General food processing
SUS316 & SUS316L
Corrosion-Resistant Grades
Higher molybdenum content - significantly better resistance to chlorides, salt brine, acidic marinades, and aggressive CIP chemicals. SUS316 is the standard carbon variant; SUS316L (low-carbon) offers superior weldability and intergranular corrosion resistance after welding. Both available. Recommended for seafood, marinated products, and high-washdown environments.
Seafood processing Salt brine environments Marinated meat & poultry Aggressive CIP Acidic product contact
SUS302
High-Strength Grade
Higher carbon content provides greater hardness and spring-back resistance. For high-tension conveying applications or where belt elongation under load is a concern. Good corrosion resistance for dry or mild-environment applications.
High-tension conveying Heavy-load lines Dry food processing Custom on request

Frequently Asked Questions

Sample review is available for all grades before bulk production when required — 7–14 working days at production cost for standard pitch and wire diameter combinations. Standard grades: SUS304, SUS316, SUS316L, SUS302. Custom grades available on request. All wire is drawn in-house on YIYI Mesh Belt’s self-developed drawing equipment to 1,200 MPa tensile strength — higher than standard commercial wire. Higher tensile strength means stronger controlled welds, better resistance to belt elongation under load, and longer service life in high-cycle food processing applications.
Left-turn and right-turn radius belts are mirror images of each other. The inner radius (smaller pitch) is on opposite sides. They are not interchangeable — a left-turn belt cannot be installed in a right-turn conveyor. Always specify the direction on your drawing or enquiry. For conveyor systems with both L and R turns, order each configuration separately.
SUS316 and SUS316L both contain molybdenum which gives them superior corrosion resistance over SUS304. The key difference: SUS316L is the low-carbon variant (carbon ≤0.03% vs ≤0.08% for SUS316). SUS316L has better weldability and resists intergranular corrosion after welding - important for robotic-welded conveyor belts in aggressive environments. Choose SUS316 for general corrosion-resistant applications (salt, seafood, acid marinades). Choose SUS316L when the belt will face repeated CIP washdown with chloride-based chemicals and weld-zone integrity is critical. Both grades outperform SUS304 in any chloride or brine environment.
