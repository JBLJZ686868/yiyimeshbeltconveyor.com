Self-Stacking Belt Structure Explained

A self-stacking belt works as part of the spiral system. Side plate height, rod position, belt width, stacking clearance, and airflow path must be reviewed together. This page explains the structure buyers should confirm before quotation or replacement.

Structure Logic

Why Self-Stacking Belts Need System Review

The belt carries product and also helps form the vertical stacking path. A small structural change can affect airflow, tracking, layer clearance, and replacement fit.

Side Plate Geometry

Side plate height, shape, and spacing affect stacking stability and product clearance inside the spiral system.

Rod and Mesh Layout

Rod position, mesh pitch, and support surface influence load support, airflow, and cleaning behavior.

System Fit

Drum diameter, turning direction, belt path, and drive components must be matched to the original or OEM design.

Checklist

Information Needed for Review

Dimensions

Overall width, pitch, rod diameter, side plate height, and belt length or tower layout.

Application

Product type, temperature, load, washdown condition, airflow need, and operation speed.

Replacement Clues

Old belt photos, worn edge details, sprocket or drum photos, and any previous failure symptoms.
