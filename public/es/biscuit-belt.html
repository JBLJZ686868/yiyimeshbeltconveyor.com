What Is a Rolled Baking Belt?
Z-Spiral Wire — Compressed Flat by Precision Rolling

A Rolled Baking Belt starts as a Z-form spiral wire mesh. It is then passed through a large precision rolling machine that compresses the structure flat. The result is an ultra-thin, smooth belt surface with consistent geometry across the full width and length.

The flat, low-profile surface is critical for biscuit production: dough pieces lie flat without tipping, heat distributes evenly from below, and the belt runs at high speed without product displacement.

1
Z-spiral formed — wire looped into Z-form, interlocked across belt width
2
Precision rolling — in-house large roller compresses mesh flat to <3mm profile
3
Straight-tracking test — 100% of belts run under tension before shipment
4
Full-length packing — rolled onto steel core, moisture-barrier wrap, wooden export crate

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Standard wire diameter is 1.5mm. This is the industry specification for high-speed biscuit and cracker tunnel ovens. All 1.5mm wire is drawn in-house by YIYI Mesh Belt on self-developed drawing equipment to a tensile strength of 1,200 MPa — significantly higher than standard commercial wire. SUS316 and SUS316L available on request.
YIYI Mesh Belt produces each Rolled Baking Belt as a single continuous piece at full customer length — no mid-belt joints during production. A mid-belt joint creates a visible seam in the mesh pattern, a potential weak point under oven tension, and a surface irregularity that can mark biscuits. One-piece production eliminates all three risks. The only joint is the end-connection joint where the belt loops back on itself, controlled to your specification.
After rolling, the belt can be placed on YIYI Mesh Belt’s in-house tracking test station and run under tension. The test verifies tracking behaviour across the belt length. Belts with tracking deviation are corrected or rejected. A tracking test record or video can be prepared before shipment when required.
