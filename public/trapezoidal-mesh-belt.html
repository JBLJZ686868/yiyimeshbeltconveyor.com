Structure & Engineering Logic

Trapezoidal Spiral — Built for Turning

A standard balanced weave or compound weave belt has equal pitch across its full width. This prevents it from navigating curves without bunching or stretching. Trapezoidal mesh belt uses wider pitch on the outer edge and narrower pitch toward the centre — creating natural lateral flexibility that allows tight radius turns without distortion.

↻
Tight Turning Radius
Trapezoidal spiral geometry allows the belt to navigate curves with small turning radii that standard rectangular mesh cannot handle.
🧱
Flat Product Surface
Despite lateral flexibility, the belt surface remains flat. Products do not tip, slide, or displace during curved conveying at normal speeds.
⚲
Spiral System Compatible
Designed for spiral freezer and proofing tower systems where the belt must travel a helical path. Standard spiral freezer belt alternative.

Hygienic Design
Open mesh allows full drainage and air circulation. SUS304/316 stainless throughout. CIP washdown compatible. Hygiene-sensitive approved grades available.
🗡
Low Maintenance
No fabric, no plastic components. All-metal stainless construction. Long service life in cold, wet, and high-wash environments.
🔨
In-House Production

OEM Engineering Confirmation

Before Production, the Turning Geometry Must Be Confirmed

For a trapezoidal mesh belt, the key risk is not only wire diameter or belt width. The belt must match the conveyor curve radius, sprocket or drum contact, edge format, and product support requirement. YIYI reviews these points before production so the belt can turn smoothly without binding, bunching, or edge overload.

↻
Curve Radius Review
Inner radius, outer radius, belt width, and conveyor path are checked together. If the radius is too tight for the requested width, we adjust pitch logic before release.
⚙
Drive Contact Matching
Sprocket, drum, or guide rail contact must match the belt pitch and edge format. This helps prevent jumping, edge rubbing, and uneven pull during curved conveying.
📏
Width and Pitch Control
Overall width, inner pitch, outer pitch, wire diameter, and edge straightness are checked against the drawing or sample before the belt moves to final inspection.
🧹
Food-Line Surface Check
Weld points, burr condition, open area, and cleanability are reviewed for freezing, cooling, proofing, washing, and curved food conveyor applications.
